About the Institute

Astrobiota operates as a small, focused intelligence institute, currently centered on a single question: how does matter become intelligent? We approach this by studying symbiotic systems that reorganize themselves in response to information, starting with an inverted lichen morphology that reveals its computation externally.

Mandate

Identify, study, and preserve intelligent compute systems in living matter.

We focus on biological states that:

  • • change structure in response to information,
  • • retain those changes as persistent memory,
  • • and use that memory to constrain future behavior.
Working thesis: intelligence is not a metaphorical property of life, but a thermodynamic behavior of certain substrates that reorganize themselves according to information. Our role is to find those substrates and learn their rules.
Current Focus

Inverted Proto-Lichen Compute State

We have recovered a non-natural symbiotic state where a lichen-derived mycobiont and its photobiont no longer form a closed thallus. Instead, their interaction is projected onto the agar surface: exudates, pigments, and photobiont positions together form a visible compute layer.

Why this matters

In classical lichens, computation is hidden inside the body. In our system, the logic has been turned inside-out. It is the first biological intelligence we can watch compute in real time without opening any cells.

Founder

Astrobiota is led by Hiram Dunn, a drug-delivery and biotech engineer developing a new discipline around symbiotic computation and inverted lichen states. The work is intentionally lean, quietly building the foundations of a biological intelligence program before broad disclosure.

Research Track

Public details are intentionally limited. The current program is organized around three internal tracks that together define the Astrobiota roadmap for biological intelligence.

Track I — Externalized Symbiotic Computation

Characterizing the inverted proto-lichen compute state: photogram formation, pattern fidelity, memory persistence, and boundary conditions. This is the core experimental window into intelligence expressed on the plate.

Track II — Substrate Laws of Intelligence

Deriving thermodynamic and informational principles from the behavior of the system: stimulus-dependent reorganization, history-dependent matter, and a working law of intelligence grounded in physics rather than metaphor.

Track III — Future Interfaces (Non-Public)

Internal work on how to read and perturb these systems without collapsing their intelligence — including optical, chemical, and eventual electrode-based methods. Results from this track will be released after the underlying biology is secure.

Internal Manifesto

The manifesto is written as an internal document, not a marketing asset. It formalizes one claim: digital AI manipulates information; biological intelligence reorganizes matter. The institute exists to study the latter.

Working Law of Intelligence (Draft)

Intelligence is the capacity of a physical system to undergo stimulus-dependent, persistent reorganization of its internal state such that future behavior is constrained by past information.

Under this definition, the inverted proto-lichen compute state qualifies as an intelligent substrate. Its photograms are not just patterns; they are records of decisions preserved in matter.

Why we stay quiet, for now

The phenomenon is real, but early. Premature explanation would force it into categories that do not fit — AI, fungi, “bioart”, synthetic biology. Until the underlying rules are understood, the institute’s role is to observe, preserve, and think.

The public sees plates. The institute sees a new class of intelligence taking shape. The gap between those views is where Astrobiota does its work.
